# Adaptive Learning Platforms
*Type: tool category*

## Overview
Adaptive learning platforms are educational systems that adjust the content, pace, and sequence of instruction based on individual learner performance. They range from simple rule‑based systems that modify difficulty to advanced AI‑driven platforms like Squirrel AI that decompose subjects into granular knowledge points and create fully personalized learning paths. ## Strategic Value - Through Education Lens
**PRIMARY: Education Transformation Value**
- **Learning Game Potential:** Adaptive mechanics can be embedded directly into learning games—difficulty scaling, content sequencing, and feedback loops that adjust to player performance, creating a truly personalized gaming experience that teaches without frustration or boredom.
- **Concept Simplification:** For a 6‑year‑old: "It's like a game that gets easier when you're stuck and harder when you're doing great, so it's always just right." For an 11‑year‑old: "It's like a smart coach that watches how you play and changes the practice drills to fix your weak spots."
- **Role Modeling:** Shows that **one‑size‑fits‑all doesn't work**—effective teaching (and parenting) requires adjusting to the individual's current level and progress.
- **Future Connection:** Adaptive systems are the foundation of personalized digital assistants and AI tutors that will be commonplace in future education and workplace training.

**For Aurora:** Adaptive platforms could train energy analysts on complex market models, adjusting difficulty based on their existing math/stats background and filling gaps before advancing to more sophisticated simulations.
**For Two Alpha:** Adaptive quantitative training could help team members strengthen specific financial modeling skills (e.g., Monte Carlo simulation, time‑series analysis) based on their current proficiency, accelerating onboarding and continuous skill development.
**Personal Network:** Understanding adaptive learning mechanics provides a framework for evaluating edtech tools and discussing personalized learning with educators and technologists.
