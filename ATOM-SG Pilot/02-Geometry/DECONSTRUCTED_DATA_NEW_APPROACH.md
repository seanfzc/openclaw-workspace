## Q21: Overlapping Quarter Circles (G5-Composite Overlap)

### Gate 0 — Solve First

**Given:**
- Two quarter circles OAC and OBD, radius 10 cm
- Shaded OBC: area = 30 cm², perimeter = 26 cm

**Solution:**
1. Perimeter OBC = OB + OC + arc BC = 26
2. 10 + 10 + arc BC = 26
3. Arc BC = 6 cm
4. Area OBC = 30 cm² (given) ✓

**Area OABCD:**
- Area Q1 = ¼ × π × 10² = 78.5 cm²
- Area Q2 = 78.5 cm²
- Area OABCD = 78.5 + 78.5 - 30 = 127 cm²

**Perimeter OABCD:**
- Arc AB = Arc CD (by symmetry)
- Arc AD = arc AB + arc BC + arc CD
- Need to calculate from geometry

### Gate 1 — Audience Constraint Check

**P5-P6 can use:**
- Fractions (¼, ½)
- π = 3.14
- Area of circle = πr²
- Circumference = 2πr

**Cannot use:**
- Algebra variables
- Trigonometry
- Advanced geometry theorems

**Labels to use:**
- "10 cm" (not "r = 10")
- "shaded area" (not "sector OBC")
- Plain language

### Gate 2 — Map Reasoning Chain

**Step 1:** Identify quarter circles OAC and OBD
- Visual: Show two overlapping quarter circles
- Both have radius 10 cm

**Step 2:** Find arc BC from perimeter
- Given: perimeter OBC = 26 cm
- OB = 10, OC = 10
- Arc BC = 26 - 20 = 6 cm
- Visual: Label arc BC = 6 cm

**Step 3:** Calculate area OABCD
- Area Q1 = ¼ × 3.14 × 100 = 78.5
- Area Q2 = 78.5
- Shaded = 30
- Total = 78.5 + 78.5 - 30 = 127
- Visual: Show calculation in panels

**Step 4:** Calculate perimeter OABCD
- Arc AD = arc AB + arc BC + arc CD
- By symmetry: arc AB = arc CD
- Total arc AD + OA + OD
- Visual: Show outer boundary

### Gate 3 — Plan Scale

**Panel 1:** Establish geometry
- Scale: 1 cm = 10 pixels
- Show overlapping quarter circles
- Radius = 10 cm (100 pixels)

**Panel 2:** Calculate arc BC
- Show: 26 - 10 - 10 = 6
- Arc BC = 6 cm

**Panel 3:** Calculate area
- Show: 78.5 + 78.5 - 30 = 127

**Panel 4:** Calculate perimeter
- Show outer arc + radii

## Q22: Grid Construction (G6-Grid Construction)

### Gate 0 — Solve First

**Given:**
- Triangle ABC on 1cm grid
- Need to complete trapezium BCDE
- BC ∥ DE, AB = BE, DE = 2×BC

**Solution:**
1. Measure ∠ACB with protractor
2. Find point E such that AB = BE
3. Draw DE parallel to BC, length = 2×BC

### Gate 1 — Audience Constraint Check

**P5-P6 can use:**

- Length comparison

**Visual strategy:**

- Extend line from B
- Make BE = AB
- Visual: Compass arc or measurement

**Step 3:** Draw DE parallel to BC

- Visual: Show parallel construction

## Q24: Angle Chasing (G8-Angle Chasing)

### Gate 0 — Solve First

**Given:**
- Rhombus ABCD
- Trapezium ADEF, AF ∥ DE
- ∠DFE = 21°, ∠BCD = 108°, ∠DAB = 33°
- Find x and y

**Solution:**
1. Rhombus: opposite angles equal, adjacent supplementary
2. ∠BAD = 108° (opposite ∠BCD)
3. ∠ADC = 72° (supplementary)
4. Use parallel lines AF ∥ DE
5. Calculate angles x and y

### Gate 1 — Audience Constraint Check

**P5-P6 can use:**
- Angle properties of rhombus
- Parallel line properties
- Angle sum

**Cannot use:**
- Complex algebra
- Advanced theorems

### Gate 2 — Map Reasoning Chain

**Step 1:** Identify rhombus properties
- Visual: Mark equal sides, parallel sides
- Show angle relationships

**Step 2:** Use given angles
- ∠BCD = 108° → ∠BAD = 108°
- ∠ADC = 72°
- Visual: Label all known angles

**Step 3:** Use trapezium properties
- AF ∥ DE
- Calculate angles using parallel lines
- Visual: Show angle chasing

**Step 4:** Find x and y
- Visual: Mark final answers

### Gate 3 — Plan Scale

- Clear geometric shapes
- Angle arcs visible
- Labels clear
