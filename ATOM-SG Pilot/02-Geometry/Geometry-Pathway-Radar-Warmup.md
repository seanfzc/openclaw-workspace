# Geometry Pathway Radar Warm‑up (Sample)
*3‑question recognition‑first training aligned with Geometry Taxonomy and Baseline Test*

## Overview
This is a ready‑to‑use 5‑minute warm‑up that applies the Geometry‑Specific Recognition‑First Loop. It trains rapid pattern recognition for geometry problems using the G1–G4 sub‑pathways and 18 nano‑nodes.

**Total time:** 5 minutes  
**Questions:** 3 (G1 + G2 + G4)  
**Focus:** Recognition and articulation only (no solving)

## Warm‑up Structure

| Question | Sub‑Pathway | Nano‑Node | Difficulty Zone | Time Allocation |
|----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Q1 | G1 Angle Reasoning | Use angle properties to find unknown angles (angles on straight line) | B | 2 minutes |
| Q2 | G2 Area & Perimeter | Calculate area of composite rectangular figures | B | 2 minutes |
| Q3 | G4 Properties & Classification | Calculate circumference/area of circle using π | C | 1 minute |

## Implementation Flow
1. **Display question** with diagram (if any) and allocated time countdown.
2. **Student articulates:**
   - Sub‑pathway (G1–G4)
   - Specific nano‑node (from 18‑item taxonomy)
   - Equation shadow
   - Cues that trigger that shadow
3. **Timer stops** after articulation.
4. **Immediate feedback** using unified rubric (Vague/Partial/Clear).

## Question 1: G1 Angle Reasoning

**Problem ID:** G002 (from Problem‑Pack Plan)  
**Source:** Baseline Test Framework  
**Time:** 2 minutes

### Problem Statement
> In the diagram, points A, B, and C lie on a straight line.  
> ∠ABD = 35° and ∠DBC = 85°.  
> Find ∠ABC.

*(Diagram would show straight line ABC with point D above the line, forming angles ABD and DBC)*

### Expected Articulation
**Sub‑pathway:** G1 Angle Reasoning  
**Nano‑node:** "Use angle properties to find unknown angles (angles on straight line)"  
**Equation shadow:** `∠ABD + ∠DBC = ∠ABC` (or `∠ABD + ∠DBC = 180°` if interpreting as angles on straight line)

**Cue mapping:**
1. "points A, B, and C lie on a straight line" → triggers "angles on straight line" property
2. "∠ABD = 35° and ∠DBC = 85°" → two angles given
3. "Find ∠ABC" → need to find the total angle

**Full articulation example:**
"This is a G1 Angle Reasoning problem because it involves angles on a straight line. The specific nano‑node is 'Use angle properties to find unknown angles (angles on straight line)'. The equation shadow is `∠ABD + ∠DBC = ∠ABC`. The cues are: 'points A, B, and C lie on a straight line' (straight‑line context), '∠ABD = 35° and ∠DBC = 85°' (two component angles given), 'Find ∠ABC' (total angle needed)."

### Solution (for instructor reference)
```
∠ABC = ∠ABD + ∠DBC
∠ABC = 35° + 85°
∠ABC = 120°
```

## Question 2: G2 Area & Perimeter

**Problem ID:** G011 (from Problem‑Pack Plan)  
**Source:** Baseline Test Framework  
**Time:** 2 minutes

### Problem Statement
> The figure below is made up of two rectangles.  
> Rectangle A measures 8 cm by 5 cm.  
> Rectangle B measures 4 cm by 6 cm.  
> They are joined along a 5 cm side.  
> Find the total area of the figure.

*(Diagram would show an L‑shape composed of two rectangles)*

### Expected Articulation
**Sub‑pathway:** G2 Area & Perimeter  
**Nano‑node:** "Calculate area of composite rectangular figures"  
**Equation shadow:** `total_area = area_A + area_B` where `area = length × breadth`

**Cue mapping:**
1. "figure below is made up of two rectangles" → composite rectangular figure
2. "Rectangle A measures 8 cm by 5 cm" → dimensions for area calculation
3. "Rectangle B measures 4 cm by 6 cm" → dimensions for area calculation  
4. "Find the total area" → sum of individual areas

**Full articulation example:**
"This is a G2 Area & Perimeter problem because it involves finding the area of a composite figure. The specific nano‑node is 'Calculate area of composite rectangular figures'. The equation shadow is `total_area = (8 × 5) + (4 × 6)`. The cues are: 'made up of two rectangles' (composite figure), '8 cm by 5 cm' and '4 cm by 6 cm' (dimensions for area formula), 'Find the total area' (summation required)."

### Solution (for instructor reference)
```
Area A = 8 × 5 = 40 cm²
Area B = 4 × 6 = 24 cm²
Total area = 40 + 24 = 64 cm²
```

## Question 3: G4 Properties & Classification

**Problem ID:** G024 (from Problem‑Pack Plan)  
**Source:** Baseline Test Framework  
**Time:** 1 minute

### Problem Statement
> A circle has a radius of 7 cm.  
> Using π = 22/7, find its circumference.

### Expected Articulation
**Sub‑pathway:** G4 Properties & Classification  
**Nano‑node:** "Calculate circumference/area of circle using π"  
**Equation shadow:** `C = 2 × π × r` or `C = π × d`

**Cue mapping:**
1. "circle has a radius of 7 cm" → radius given, triggers circle formula
2. "Using π = 22/7" → value of π specified
3. "find its circumference" → asks for circumference, not area

**Full articulation example:**
"This is a G4 Properties & Classification problem because it involves circle properties. The specific nano‑node is 'Calculate circumference/area of circle using π'. The equation shadow is `C = 2 × π × r`. The cues are: 'circle has a radius of 7 cm' (radius given), 'Using π = 22/7' (π value specified), 'find its circumference' (circumference formula needed)."

### Solution (for instructor reference)
```
C = 2 × π × r
C = 2 × (22/7) × 7
C = 2 × 22
C = 44 cm
```

- **Intermediate:** Provide nano‑node options (from 18‑item list) as multiple choice

### Integration with GeoBot

- Each has pre‑defined nano‑node and equation shadow mapping
- Can be extended to other items in the 25‑item bank

### Extension to Full Loop

1. **Shadow:** Write equation setup
2. **Solve:** Perform calculations
3. **Check:** Verify answer reasonableness
