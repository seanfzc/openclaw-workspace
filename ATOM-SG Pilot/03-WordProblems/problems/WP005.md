equationShadow: Looks like Part-Whole (fraction of total) but is actually Before-After (sequential changes from remainder)
traps: Students see "fraction" and immediately do Part-Whole; miss the "remainder" keyword indicating sequential change
question: A baker made 360 cupcakes. He sold 1/4 of them in the morning and 2/3 of the remainder in the afternoon. How many cupcakes did he have left?
answer: 90 cupcakes
solutionSteps:
  - Morning: 360 × 1/4 = 90 sold, remainder = 270
  - Afternoon: 270 × 2/3 = 180 sold
  - Left: 360 - 90 - 180 = 90 cupcakes
  - Or: 270 × 1/3 = 90 cupcakes
articulationRubric:
  level1: "Mentions fractions and selling cupcakes"
  level2: "Identifies two selling periods but may treat as simultaneous fractions"
  level3: "Articulates: This is Before-After Change (not Part-Whole), 1/4 of original first, then 2/3 of remainder, sequential changes"
