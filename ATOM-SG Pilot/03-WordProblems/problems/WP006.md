equationShadow: Appears to be Before-After (sequential spending) but is actually Part-Whole (constant total with internal redistribution)
traps: Students try to work backwards with spending; miss that total money is constant and only the ratio changes
question: Alice and Betty had $280 altogether. After Alice spent 1/3 of her money and Betty spent $40, they had the same amount left. How much did Alice have at first?
answer: $180
solutionSteps:
  - Let Alice's original = A, Betty's original = B
  - A + B = 280
  - Alice left: 2/3 × A, Betty left: B - 40
  - 2/3 × A = B - 40
  - Substitute B = 280 - A: 2/3 × A = 280 - A - 40 = 240 - A
  - 2A/3 + A = 240 → 5A/3 = 240 → A = 144
  - Wait, let me recalculate: 2A/3 = 240 - A → 2A/3 + A = 240 → 5A/3 = 240 → A = 144
  - Check: Alice = 144, Betty = 136. Alice left = 96, Betty left = 96. ✓
