equationShadow: After change, ratio becomes m:n, use ratio to find original amounts before change
traps: Students apply ratio to original instead of final amounts; confuse before/after relationships
question: The ratio of red balls to blue balls in a bag was 3:5. After removing 12 red balls, the ratio became 1:3. How many red balls were there at first?
answer: 27 red balls
solutionSteps:
  - Let original red = 3u, blue = 5u (ratio 3:5)
  - After removing 12 red: red = 3u - 12, blue = 5u
  - New ratio: (3u - 12) : 5u = 1 : 3
  - So 3(3u - 12) = 5u → 9u - 36 = 5u → 4u = 36 → u = 9
  - Original red = 3 × 9 = 27 balls
