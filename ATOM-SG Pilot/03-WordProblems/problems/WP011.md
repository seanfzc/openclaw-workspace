equationShadow: Appears to be ratio problem but requires understanding of constant difference or sum with Before-After logic
traps: Students treat as simple ratio without accounting for the change; miss that difference between quantities stays constant
question: The ratio of Alan's age to Ben's age is 4:7. In 6 years, the ratio will be 2:3. How old is Alan now?
answer: 24 years old
solutionSteps:
  - Let Alan = 4u, Ben = 7u (ratio 4:7)
  - In 6 years: Alan = 4u + 6, Ben = 7u + 6
  - New ratio: (4u + 6) : (7u + 6) = 2 : 3
  - So 3(4u + 6) = 2(7u + 6)
  - 12u + 18 = 14u + 12
  - 6 = 2u → u = 3
  - Alan now = 4 × 3 = 24 years old
