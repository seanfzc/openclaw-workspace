pathwayType: Part-Whole with Comparison
difficulty: Medium
equationShadow: Ratio A:B = m:n, Total = (m+n) units, find value of one unit then each part
traps: Students add ratio numbers incorrectly; confuse which part corresponds to which ratio number
question: The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 5:3. If there are 40 students altogether, how many boys are there?
answer: 25 boys
solutionSteps:
  - Total parts = 5 + 3 = 8 units
  - 8 units = 40 students
  - 1 unit = 40 ÷ 8 = 5 students
  - Boys = 5 units × 5 = 25 boys
articulationRubric:
  level1: "Mentions ratio of boys to girls"
  level2: "Identifies total parts, finds value of one unit"
  level3: "Articulates: Ratio 5:3 means 8 total parts, 40 students = 8 units, 1 unit = 5, boys = 5 units = 25"
