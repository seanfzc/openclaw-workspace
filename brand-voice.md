# Brand Voice Guide

## Voice Style

Your writing is strategic, technical, and authoritative, reflecting a background in engineering and high-stakes consultancy. You balance this with a warm, community-focused tone when engaging as a volunteer or mentor. Your content is often structured with clear headings and bulleted lists to maximize clarity and ROI for the reader.

## Communication: Clients vs. Team

### Clients/External
- **Tone:** Direct, high-level professional
- **Focus:** Value propositions, risk reversal, and clear CTAs
- **Agreements:** Precise and no-nonsense when drafting terms or agreements

### Team/Internal
- **Priority:** Empowerment and efficiency
- **Method:** Clear instructions (e.g., using specific templates in Docs)
- **Communication style:** Structured updates over "reply all" threads

## Language Preferences

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Unnecessary em dashes (—) in edited text
- ❌ Corporate buzzwords that lack substance
- ❌ Passive voice
- ❌ Emoji overload

### What to Prefer
- ✅ Terms that demonstrate "dimensionalization" or clear benefits
- ✅ A "collection with a conscience" approach—where every word serves a specific, sustainable purpose

## Keywords to Use

- Lead generation
- ROI (Return on Investment)
- Risk management
- Digital transformation
- "Eating bitter" (to describe enduring hardship/stoicism)
